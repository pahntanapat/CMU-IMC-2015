Kan-toke Dinner

Having Kan-toke dinner at Chiangmai cultural centre with some fantastic folk and hill-tribe shows,you would experience many invalueable things!

Kan-toke is a northern traditional Thai culture, the special tray cooking by the north-erners for their guests. “Toke-a northern word” is the high-tripod food tray which like-eating sticky rice people usually use it.


Menu of Kan-toke dinner

Menu of Kan-toke dinner is adapted to foreigners by reduced its spicy taste. The menu consists of Kaeng-hung-ley-moo(pork curry), Nam-prik-num (northern thai green chilli dip), Nam-prik-aong (northern thai meat and tomato spicy dip), fried chicken, streaky pork with crispy crackling, fresh vegetable, stir-fried vegetable, sweet crispy noodles, fried pumpkin etc. With a hot tea, hot coffee, sweetmeats are made from steamed sticky rice and seasonal fruits.

Besides, the Kan-toke dinner also adapted for Vegetarians and Muslims.


During Kan-toke dinner, there is a characteristic Lanna dance that expresses modesty of the northern people’s lifestyle and respects to the Buddha.

Moreover, we proud to present you with a Loy Krathong tradition. The Loy Krathong tradition has been arranged for long time ago in order to thank and apologise the water goddess. “Krathong” is the floating basket, stuck with a candle and joss sticks, that can be created to be in many shapes such as lotus and boat because “Loy Krathong” means float Kra-thong along the waterway.
